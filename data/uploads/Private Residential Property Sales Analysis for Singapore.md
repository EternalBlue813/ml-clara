# Private Residential Property Sales Analysis for Singapore
## Quarterly Trends and Market Insights (1999 Q4 - 2025 Q2)

---

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis examines over **25 years of quarterly private residential property transaction data** in Singapore, spanning from Q4 1999 to Q2 2025. The dataset encompasses **568,837 total transactions** across three main categories: **New Sale**, **Resale**, and **Sub Sale**. The analysis reveals significant market cycles, structural shifts in buyer preferences, and actionable insights for real estate professionals.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. **Historical Market Cycles**

The Singapore private residential property market has experienced **distinct boom-and-bust cycles** over the past 25 years:

- **Peak Quarter**: Q2 2007 recorded the highest transaction volume with **14,797 units**, driven by strong economic growth and speculative activity before the Global Financial Crisis.
- **Trough Quarter**: Q1 2003 saw the lowest activity with only **1,407 units**, during the SARS outbreak and post-dot-com bubble recovery period.
- **Average Quarterly Volume**: Approximately **5,523 units** per quarter across the entire period.

### 2. **Recent Market Performance (2020-2025)**

The market has shown **resilience and recovery** following the COVID-19 pandemic:

- **2020 Q2**: Sharp decline to **2,664 units** during circuit breaker measures
- **2021**: Strong recovery with peak at **9,083 units** in Q3 2021
- **2024-2025**: Market stabilization around **5,000-7,500 units** per quarter
- **2025 Q2**: **5,128 units** transacted, showing moderate activity levels

### 3. **Market Composition Shifts**

The market composition has evolved significantly:

**Current Market (2025 Q2)**:
- **Resale**: 71.1% (3,647 units) - **Dominant segment**
- **New Sale**: 23.6% (1,212 units)
- **Sub Sale**: 5.2% (269 units)

**Key Observations**:
- **Resale market** has become the primary driver of transaction volume
- **New Sale** activity heavily concentrated in **uncompleted properties** (1,204 units vs. only 8 completed units in Q2 2025)
- **Sub Sale** activity has declined significantly from historical peaks (e.g., 1,892 units in Q2 2007)

### 4. **Seasonality Patterns**

Analysis of quarterly patterns across all years reveals **moderate seasonality**:

- **Q2 and Q3**: Highest average volumes (**6,038** and **6,003** units respectively)
- **Q1 and Q4**: Slightly lower volumes (**5,072** and **4,995** units respectively)
- **Implication**: Mid-year periods (Q2-Q3) tend to see stronger market activity

### 5. **Year-over-Year Growth Volatility**

The market exhibits **high volatility** in growth rates:

- **Extreme positive growth**: +305% YoY in Q2 2009 (post-GFC recovery)
- **Extreme negative growth**: -70% YoY in Q1 2003 (SARS impact)
- **Recent trends**: More moderate fluctuations with YoY growth ranging from -50% to +70% in 2024-2025

---

## Market Trends by Transaction Type

### **New Sale Transactions**

**Historical Pattern**:
- Dominated by **uncompleted properties** (off-plan purchases)
- Peak periods: 2007 (pre-GFC), 2009-2013 (post-GFC recovery), 2021 (post-COVID recovery)
- Recent surge: Q4 2024 saw **3,404 uncompleted units** sold, indicating strong developer launches

**Current Status**:
- **Completed new sales** have declined to minimal levels (only 8 units in Q2 2025)
- **Uncompleted sales** remain the primary new sale channel (1,204 units in Q2 2025)

### **Resale Transactions**

**Historical Pattern**:
- Most **stable and consistent** transaction type
- Peak: Q2 2007 with **7,776 units**
- Maintained strong volumes even during market downturns

**Current Status**:
- **Dominant market segment** at 71.1% of total transactions
- Steady volume around **3,500-3,800 units** per quarter in 2024-2025
- Reflects mature market with active secondary trading

### **Sub Sale Transactions**

**Historical Pattern**:
- Highly **speculative** in nature
- Peak: Q2 2007 with **1,892 units** during market frenzy
- Declined significantly after cooling measures

**Current Status**:
- Minimal activity at **5.2%** of market (269 units in Q2 2025)
- Reflects successful government cooling measures and reduced speculation

## Recommendations for Real Estate Agents

### **For Buyer Advisory**

1. **Timing Considerations**:
   - **Q2-Q3** historically shows higher transaction volumes, indicating more market liquidity and choice
   - Current market (2025) shows **stabilization** after recent volatility - favorable for buyers seeking stability

2. **Segment Focus**:
   - **Resale market** offers the **widest selection** and most active trading (71% of market)
   - **New launches** remain attractive for buyers seeking uncompleted properties, especially after strong Q4 2024 launches

3. **Market Cycle Positioning**:
   - Current volumes (5,000-7,500 units/quarter) are **below historical peak** but **above historical average**
   - Market is in a **moderate activity phase**, not overheated nor depressed

### **For Seller Advisory**

1. **Optimal Timing**:
   - **Q2-Q3** periods typically see higher transaction volumes, potentially offering better liquidity
   - Monitor **YoY growth trends** - positive growth quarters may indicate improving sentiment

2. **Market Positioning**:
   - **Resale segment** is highly active - sellers can expect reasonable market depth
   - Competition from **new launches** should be considered, especially when developer activity surges

3. **Pricing Strategy**:
   - Current market is **balanced** - neither extreme seller's nor buyer's market
   - Historical volatility suggests importance of **realistic pricing** to capture transaction windows

### **For Investment Advisory**

1. **Market Cycle Awareness**:
   - Singapore property market exhibits **clear cyclical patterns** (7-10 year major cycles visible)
   - Current phase appears **mid-cycle** - neither peak nor trough

2. **Segment Selection**:
   - **Resale properties** offer liquidity and established neighborhoods
   - **Uncompleted new sales** offer potential capital appreciation but carry completion risk

3. **Risk Management**:
   - **Sub-sale activity** at historic lows indicates reduced speculative pressure - healthier market fundamentals
   - Government cooling measures remain effective - advise clients on regulatory environment

### **For Market Monitoring**

1. **Key Indicators to Track**:
   - **New Sale (Uncompleted) volumes** - leading indicator of developer confidence and future supply
   - **YoY growth rates** - early warning of market overheating or cooling
   - **Resale/New Sale ratio** - indicates market maturity and buyer preferences

2. **Warning Signs**:
   - **Rapid YoY growth** (>100%) historically precedes corrections
   - **Sharp decline in resale volumes** may indicate liquidity concerns
   - **Surge in sub-sale activity** could signal speculative excess

---

## Conclusion

The Singapore private residential property market demonstrates **resilience and maturity** after 25 years of evolution. The current market (2025) is characterized by:

- **Resale dominance** (71% of transactions)
- **Moderate transaction volumes** (5,000-7,500 units/quarter)
- **Reduced speculation** (sub-sale at 5%)
- **Stable growth patterns** compared to historical extremes

For real estate professionals, this environment offers **balanced opportunities** across buyer and seller advisory, with emphasis on the robust resale market and selective engagement with new launches. Understanding these historical patterns and current dynamics enables more informed client recommendations and strategic positioning.
